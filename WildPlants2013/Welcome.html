Wild plants
of Ontario

 
 

This course explores the remarkable diversity of terrestrial plants in the Land Between - the region surrounding Peterborough, encompassing the ecological transition between Lake Ontario to the south and the Shield to the north. We use the variety of habitats that occur here (including wetlands, savannahs, alvars, and rich forests) to examine plant diversity across ecological communities. In the second half of the course, our focus shifts to Algonquin Park, where we use experimental approaches to examine functional diversity. There, the focus is mostly on floral function and the ecology of reproductive systems. Students will gain hands-on experience with plants by conducting their own projects using our regional flora.
